How do they work?

On the hard drive platter, there is a thin coating of magnetic grains. The alignment of the grains represents a binary "0" or "1". As the platter spins and the grains pass under the read/write head, the small magnet in the head can check or change the alignment of the grains. Different types of magnets in the read/write head and various chemical compounds on the platter can affect the speed and amount of data stored. Multiple read/write heads and platters can be stacked to have even more storage.
